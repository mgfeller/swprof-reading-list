Kanban

  • Visualize work
  • Organize work
  • Complete work
  • Transparent work progress
  • Establish value stream
  • Limit work-in-progress (WIP)
  • Pull-based system
  • Throughput instead of capacity
  • Everybody needs to know what really is going on!
  • Avoid unplanned work.
  • Unplanned work is not free, it comes at the expense of planned work.
  • Left unchecked, technical dept will ensure that the only work that gets done is unplanned work.
  • When introducing Kanban, do not change the way you work. Rather, analyse how work is done, find the value stream, and start visualizing.
  • WIP Limit: The amount of work you can handle at any given time.
  • Important: pull-based system! Push-based: other people telling you what to do and when to do it.
  • Capacity - quote (p49 PK): "Like traffic, work does not fit. Work flows.

Work-in-progress - Zeigarnik Effect

The adult's brain has a 90% chance of being preoccupied with unfinished business.

  • Remember interrupted or incomplete thoughts or actions over those that have been seen through to completion.
  • Brain has a tendency to seek out patterns to process meaning, it becomes preoccupied with missing pieces of information.
  • Decreases productivity and increases opportunity for error.
Personal work is messy.
  • Quote: "Personal Kanban is an information radiator for your work."
  • Simple value stream: Ready - Doing - Done
  • WIP Limit can be any low number you feel comfortable with, e.g. 3.

  • SWB: subjective well-being (p118). Annotate finished task with an indicator for this.
