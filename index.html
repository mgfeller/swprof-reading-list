The Zeigarnik Effect

The adult's brain has a 90% chance of being preoccupied with unfinished business.

  • Remember interrupted or incomplete thoughts or actions over those that have been seen through to completion.
  • Brain has a tendency to seek out patterns to process meaning, it becomes preoccupied with missing pieces of information.
  • Decreases productivity and increases opportunity for error.
  • p33 in PK

Kanban as a Permission Giver

Kanban is not a software development lifecycle methodology or an approach to project management. It requires that some process is already in place so that Kanban can be applied to incrementally change the underlying process.

Delivery Cadence

Kanban dispenses with the time-boxed iteration and instead decouples the activities of prioritization, development and delivery.
  • p92
  • Kanban does not dispense with the notion of a regular cadence.
  • Kanban avoids dysfunction introduced by artificially forcing things into time-boxes. Example: user story over 3 sprints: analysis and test planning, development, system testing and bug fixing.
  • Kanban decouples the time it takes to create a user story from the delivery rate. While some work is complete and ready for delivery, some other work will be in progress.

Theory of constraints, The Goal , Eli Goldratt (1985)

Five focusing steps:

  1. Identify the constraint
  2. Decide how to explot the constraint
  3. Subordinate everything else in the system to the decision made in step 2.
  4. Elevate the constraint
  5. Avoid intertia; identify the next constraint and return to step 2.

Kanban and Scrum

  • Kanban is not Scrum, though they are both work methods.
  • Scrum and Kanban can be combined (mainly use Kanban to visualize workflow?)
  • 3 chunks of differences:
    • Scheduling, iteration, and cadence. Kanban, there are no required time boxes or iterations. Evolve limits to keep flow steady and efficient.
    • Roles and responsibilities: k: no specific roles and responsibilities.
    • The board itself: similar to Scrum, with one vital difference: max number of items per column is limited
  • Scrum and Kanban are both iterative work systems that rely on process flows and aim to reduce waste.
  • Kanban is a visual management method that was developed by Hirotaka Takeuchi and Ikujiro Nonaka in the “New New Product Development Game.”
  • Kanban works well when used along side Scrum or any other Agile method. Basically, Kanban can be applied to visualize and improve the flow of work, regardless of the methodology being used to do the work.
  • https://leankit.com/learn/kanban/scrum-kanban/

Improvement Kata

  • "kaizen" = "improvement"
  • Typically implemented as Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA)
  • PDCA: Toyota Kata, p133
  • Plan: define problem and develop potential solutions
  • Do: Implement best solution
  • Check: Evaluate results to see if solution worked
  • Act
    • solution worked: standardize
    • solution did not work: return to planning step
  • kaizen: a Japanese business philosophy of continuous improvement of working practices, personal efficiency, etc.

Improvement Kata

Improvement Kata: 5 Questions

  1. What is the target condition? (The challenge)
  2. What is the actual condition now?
  3. What obstacles are now preventing you from reaching the target condition?
    Which ones are you addressing now?
  4. What is your next step? (Start of next PDCA cycle)
  5. When can we go and see what we have learned from taking that step?
